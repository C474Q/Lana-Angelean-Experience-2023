Module C: Prosthetics

The first records of a prosthetic device date back to the ancient Egypt. One prosthetic toe is known as the “Greville Chester Toe”, made of linen, paper mache, and glue. Historians believed that this prosthetic was mainly used as a cosmetic rather than a functional prosthetic. There is another toe prosthetic, also believed to have existed in ancient Egypt. Compared to the Greville Chester Toe, this device was made of leather and wood and is believed to be functional.

The world wars have inspired prosthetic advances. The rate of amputees from the war has risen and thus the demand for rehabilitation and later on, prosthetic devices. These devices were made from leather, wood, metal, and glue. Upon the continuous advancement of technology, the prosthetic limb devices we see today are made of more durable and lightweight materials such as titanium, plastic, and aluminum.

Prosthetic devices have been one of the most revolutionary inventions in the field of physical therapy. Upon the advancements, these devices have helped restore the function of the loss of limbs of amputees, bringing back the lives they once had. In the future, I hope physical therapy and these prosthetic devices will be much more accessible to the masses, and hopefully will be able to mimic the function of human limbs better.

Module F: Ecosystems, Global Environmentalism Biodiversity and Climate Crisis

We can see the evidence of the change of climate and the crisis it brings along in the global temperature rise. Ice glaciers are melting, leaving no habitat for the wildlife in those areas. The melting results in the rise of sea levels. Higher sea surface temperature from global warming produces much stronger typhoons, which is why typhoons in the Philippines are getting much stronger and more frequent.
